About GSBC

Who we are

The Global Somali Bantu Council is a non-partisan, community-driven body that brings together Somali Bantu elders, youth leaders, scholars, and professionals. We work collaboratively with local community organizations, international partners, and government institutions to ensure Somali Bantu voices are heard and respected.

Our story

Somali Bantu communities have a long history of resilience in the face of discrimination, conflict, and displacement. From the river valleys of southern Somalia to refugee camps and resettlement cities around the world, our people have kept their culture, language, and identity alive.

GSBC emerged from grassroots collaboration between community leaders who recognized the need for a unified global voice to advocate for rights, dignity, and inclusion.

What we do

  • Coordinate and support Somali Bantu councils and organizations worldwide.
  • Advocate for fair representation, protection, and inclusive policies.
  • Promote education, skills development, and youth leadership.
  • Preserve and celebrate Somali Bantu culture, language, and heritage.
  • Facilitate partnerships with NGOs, governments, and international bodies.

Our direction

Mission, Vision & Core Values

Everything we do is guided by a clear mission, a hopeful vision, and values rooted in our faith, culture, and shared humanity.

Our Mission

To unite Somali Bantu communities across the world, advocate for their rights, and create sustainable pathways for education, economic stability, and social inclusion.

Our Vision

A world where Somali Bantu people live in safety, dignity, and prosperity—fully recognized as equal citizens in every country they call home, while proudly preserving their culture and identity.

Core Values

  • Unity: We believe in standing together across borders and generations.
  • Justice: We speak clearly against discrimination, exploitation, and abuse.
  • Service: Leadership means serving our communities with humility.
  • Accountability: We strive for transparent, responsible governance.
  • Culture & Faith: We honor our traditions while embracing positive change.
Our work

Programs & Departments

GSBC organizes its work through focused departments and programs that respond to real needs identified by Somali Bantu communities around the world.

Education & Youth

Education & Youth Leadership

We support students, youth leaders, and parents through tutoring initiatives, scholarship connections, and leadership training.

  • Youth leadership workshops and mentoring.
  • Support for school enrollment and language access.
  • Partnerships with universities and training centers.
Advocacy & Rights

Human Rights & Legal Advocacy

We amplify community voices in local, national, and international spaces to address discrimination, statelessness, and protection concerns.

  • Documentation of human rights concerns.
  • Engagement with policymakers and institutions.
  • Support for legal referrals through trusted partners.
Livelihoods

Economic Empowerment & Development

Our development efforts focus on employment, small business support, and community-led projects that create long-term stability.

  • Skills training and entrepreneurship support.
  • Support for cooperatives and savings groups.
  • Connections to job opportunities and partners.
Culture

Cultural Preservation & Community Life

We celebrate Somali Bantu culture through language, music, oral history, and intergenerational learning.

  • Cultural festivals and community gatherings.
  • Documentation of history, language, and traditions.
  • Support for elders and youth to learn from one another.
